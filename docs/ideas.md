# SoloDev Ideas

> Brain dump of ideas, mechanics and concepts. Nothing in this document is considered final until promoted to `vision.md`.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

- Wake up.
- Plan the day.
- Decide whether to split up or stay together.
- Explore the county.
- Encounter dynamic situations.
- Race the sunset.
- Return to base.
- Prepare.
- Survive the night.
- Wake up to a changed world.
- Repeat.

---

# Core Ideas

## Time is the currency

Everything costs time.

Examples:

- Exploring
- Searching
- Fighting
- Repairing
- Helping survivors
- Crafting
- Travelling

The player should constantly ask:

> Is this the best use of our remaining daylight?

---

## Stories > Loot

Players should leave a session remembering stories.

Not:

"I found an epic shotgun."

Instead:

"I stayed too long in the quarry because I found a truck battery and barely made it back before sunset."

---

## Natural Cooperation

Players are never forced to split up.

The game naturally creates situations where:

- splitting up is more efficient
- staying together is safer

Both should be valid strategies.

---

## Permanent County

One handcrafted county.

Not procedural.

Players slowly learn it.

Buildings stay where they are.

Replayability comes from changing situations.

---

## Dynamic Events

Events change the world.

Examples:

- Bridge collapses
- Fire spreads
- Flood blocks a road
- Military convoy passes
- Building already looted
- Bandits occupy location
- Roadblock appears
- Power outage

Events should influence future events.

---

## Dynamic Encounters

Locations contain situations.

Not loot tables.

Examples:

Gas station:
- military checkpoint
- survivor
- bandit ambush
- crashed tanker
- empty
- infected nearby

Hospital:
- military already searched
- trapped survivors
- medical cache
- temporary bandit base
- fire
- heavy infected presence

School:
- shelter
- evacuation point
- temporary medical center
- collapsed roof
- abandoned supplies

Every visit should tell a different story.

---

## Continuity

The world remembers.

Examples:

- Burned buildings stay burned.
- Destroyed bridge stays destroyed.
- Vehicles remain where players leave them.
- Looted locations remain looted.
- Cleared areas remain safer for some time.

---

# Exploration

Third-person exploration.

No menu-based daytime gameplay.

Players physically explore locations.

Exploration should create tension rather than simply consume time.

The objective is not to loot everything.

The objective is to make it home.

---

# Night

Night changes the rules.

The county belongs to the infected.

The goal is to return before sunset.

Going outside should always be possible, but feel like a desperate last resort.

The infected become significantly more dangerous after dark through speed, aggression, climbing, screeching and numbers.

Night should feel fundamentally different rather than simply spawning more enemies.

---

# Infected

Current inspiration:

I Am Legend (movie + book).

Current direction:

- infected humans
- virus-controlled
- lore is placeholder for now
- mostly dormant during daylight
- extremely dangerous at night
- UV light repels them
- noise attracts them
- clearing nests during the day reduces local night danger

They are not the only threat.

---

# Other Threats

MVP threats:

- infected
- hostile survivors / bandits
- fire
- flooding
- time

Future consideration:

- hunger

Avoid adding unnecessary survival systems.

---

# Combat

Combat should be:

Easy to execute.

Hard to choose.

Every fight costs:

- time
- ammunition
- safety
- attention

The interesting decision is:

> Should we fight?

Not:

> Can we fight?

Weapon progression should come from meaningful upgrades instead of numerical stat increases.

Examples:

- bayonet
- suppressor
- red dot sight
- flashlight
- extended magazine
- UV attachment

Avoid RPG-style weapon stats.

---

# Noise

Potential future mechanic.

Noise sources:

- guns
- generators
- vehicles
- chainsaws
- explosions

Noise attracts danger.

Avoid stealth meters.

Players should naturally learn this.

---

# Atmosphere

Low-poly.

High atmosphere.

Focus on:

- fog
- lighting
- shadows
- weather
- silhouettes
- sound

Art supports gameplay.

Not the opposite.

---

# Base

Current favorite concept:

Apartment building.

Reasons:

- naturally expandable
- easy scope
- memorable
- vertical progression
- becomes "home"

Progression example:

Apartment 302
↓

303
↓

304
↓

Entire hallway
↓

Entire floor
↓

Roof

Functional rooms:

- workshop
- kitchen
- medical room
- storage
- ammunition room
- survivor quarters
- generator room
- radio room

Potential future upgrades:

- UV floodlights
- rooftop greenhouse
- water collection
- solar panels
- barricaded stairwells

Decorations are low priority.

---

# Vehicles

MVP:

- UAZ

Ideas:

Working vehicles are rare.

Finding one is exciting.

Vehicle may require:

- fuel
- battery
- keys
- repairs

Players create their own objectives.

Example:

Find UAZ.
↓

Need battery.
↓

Need fuel.
↓

Need keys.
↓

Escape before sunset.

---

# Loot

Current MVP loot:

- weapons
- ammunition
- medicine
- food
- fuel
- batteries
- UV equipment
- crafting materials
- weapon attachments

Avoid rarity systems.

Items should be memorable because of what they do, not because of their color.

---

# Progression

Current direction:

- Base progression
- Equipment progression
- Weapon upgrades
- Player knowledge

Avoid:

- player levels
- skill trees
- XP systems

---

# Sunset

Sunset should create tension naturally.

No giant countdown UI.

Players notice:

- longer shadows
- darker sky
- birds disappear
- lighting changes
- distant howls

The player should instinctively think:

"We need to go."

---

# World Feel

The county should feel alive.

The world changes regardless of the player's actions.

Players should feel like they are surviving inside an existing world.

Not the center of it.
