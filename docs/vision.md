# Vision

> Every morning we decide what we'll risk. Every night we discover what it cost.

## High Concept

A 1–4 player cooperative survival game where friends leave the safety of their apartment base each morning to scavenge a persistent county before sunset. Time is the most valuable resource. The goal is simple: survive one more day.

## Core Pillars

- Time is the primary currency.
- Permanent county, dynamic situations.
- Stories over systems.
- Combat creates decisions, not kill counts.
- The player should fear being outside after sunset.
- Art supports gameplay, not the other way around.

## Design Philosophy

Every mechanic should create interesting decisions.

The player should constantly trade:
- time
- safety
- resources
- information

The game should generate stories rather than scripted moments.

The world should feel believable instead of gamey.

If a mechanic does not improve player stories, it probably does not belong.

## World

The county is handcrafted and persistent. Buildings never move, but their state changes through dynamic events. Bridges collapse, buildings burn, roads flood, military convoys pass, bandits occupy locations and resources disappear. Yesterday matters.

## World Simulation

The county evolves naturally over time.

Buildings stay in the same place, but their state changes through events. Fires spread, roads become blocked, bridges collapse, military activity shifts, bandits occupy abandoned buildings and previously looted locations remain looted.

The player should feel like the world continues moving even when they are not there.

## Exploration

Players freely decide whether to stay together or split up. Every expedition is a tradeoff between efficiency and safety. Buildings are filled with dynamic encounters rather than static loot. The goal is not to maximize loot, but to make good decisions under uncertainty.

## Night

Night changes the rules. The world belongs to the infected. Players are expected to return before sunset, prepare their apartment base, craft, organize, repair defenses and survive until morning. Being caught outside after dark should feel terrifying.

## Base

The base is a single apartment building that gradually becomes a fortress. Players unlock apartments, assign functions to rooms, build workshops, kitchens, medical rooms, storage, generators and UV defenses. Expansion is functional rather than decorative.

## Combat

Combat is simple to execute but expensive to choose.

The interesting question is never:

> "Can I win this fight?"

It is:

> "Should I fight at all?"

Every fight consumes valuable resources:
- time
- ammunition
- attention
- safety

Noise should eventually become an important gameplay mechanic. Gunfire, generators and loud tools should attract danger.

## Atmosphere

Stylized low-poly visuals, strong lighting, weather, fog and sound create tension. The passage of time is shown through the world rather than UI. Sunset should naturally make players nervous.

## Non-Goals

SoloDev is intentionally **not** trying to become:

- Rust
- DayZ
- Escape from Tarkov
- Fallout settlement simulator
- A generic zombie survival game

We intentionally avoid:

- giant crafting trees
- loot rarity
- XP systems
- skill trees
- MMO progression
- endless procedural worlds
- excessive realism
- unnecessary simulation

Complexity should only exist if it creates better player stories.

## Definition of Success

Players finish a session telling stories instead of comparing loot.

The best moments come from difficult decisions, unexpected encounters and barely making it home before sunset.

Friends immediately want to queue for one more day.
